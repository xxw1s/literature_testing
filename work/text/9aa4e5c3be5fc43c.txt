The microstructure and phase composition of Ni/WC composite coatings were analyzed, and the hardness and wear resistance of Ni/WC composite coatings were tested. The results show that the HSLC coatings have good surface formation without defects such as pores or cracks, and WC is uniformly distributed in the coating. WC particles hinder the dendrite growth, and the dendrite structure in the coating is significantly refined with the increase of WC content. The main phases of the coating is Ni3Fe, and the W and C elements diffuse into the coating, forming strengthening phases such as W2C and Cr23C6. Although WC particles are decomposed, the strengthening effect of the second phase is not weakened, and the decomposed WC produces fine grain strengthening and solid solution strengthening effects, thus producing multiple strengthening mech­ anisms for the coating. With the increase of WC content, the hardness and wear resistance of the coating are gradually improved. However, when the WC content is in the range of 30–60%, the wear resistance of the coating do not present significant change. The main wear mechanisms are adhesive wear, abrasive wear and oxidation wear. 1. Introduction Surface coating technologies such as electroplating, thermal spray­ ing, vapor deposition and laser cladding have been widely applied in order to enhance the surface hardness and wear resistance of parts with the development of science and technology[1–7]. Surface coating technologies are used to form one or even multiple layers of alloy or cermet layer with specific properties on the surface of metal materials in different ways, thus metal materials present high hardness, high wear resistance, high corrosion resistance and other properties that it does not own originally [8,9]. Among many surface coating technologies, laser cladding technology with high energy laser beam could make the cladding material and substrate surface to be melted, and the molten pool form simultaneously, which prepares a metallurgical coating on the surface of the substrate through fast heating and cooling process [10,11]. Laser cladding technology has the advantages of low pollution, simple process, wide range of materials, low dilution rate, small thermal deformation, dense structure and high bonding strength [12]. Therefore, the research and application of laser cladding technology in the files of friction, corrosion and other fields are gradually increasing. At present, Ni-based coating materials are the most widely used in the research and application of laser cladding materials due to the good self-solubility and wettability of Ni-based alloy powders [13], and Nibased coatings have good toughness, heat resistance, impact resis­ tance, wear resistance and corrosion resistance [14], so it is widely used in petrochemical industry, metallurgical machinery, automobile in­ dustry, aerospace and other fields [15]. Chen et al. [16] prepared Nibased alloy coating on the surface of TC2 titanium alloy by laser clad­ ding technology, and the results showed that the microhardness of the cladding layer was significantly improved about twice than that of the substrate. Xu et al. [17] prepared Ni-based coating on the surface of aluminum bronze by laser cladding technology, and the results showed that the cladding layer had good wear resistance and could be used as an excellent wear-resistant material. Although Ni-based coatings have shown good hardness, wear resis­ tance, corrosion resistance and other properties, its present service properties are still difficult to meet the extremely harsh friction condi­ tion [19–21]. In order to make parts adapt to complex and harsh working conditions, and further improve the hardness and wear resis­ tance of parts surface, scholars usually use hard ceramics to enhance the hardness and wear resistance of Ni-based alloy coatings [22,23]. WC has become the most commonly used ceramic reinforcing particles because of its high hardness, excellent wear resistance and low cost properties [24]. Although the addition of WC can improve the performance of the coating to some extent, the distribution of WC particles in the coating is uneven due to the long holding time of the molten pool and the serious thermal decomposition of WC when using the traditional low-speed laser cladding to prepare Ni/WC composite coating, which is easy to produce stress concentration, crack initiation and reduce the fracture toughness and wear resistance of the coating [25–28]. Therefore, an advanced manufacturing technology is urgently needed to solve the problems of WC thermal decomposition and coating cracking. In recent years, Fraunhofer Institute for Laser Technology and RWTH Aachen University had proposed the idea of HSLC technology and car­ ried out research, and HSLC technology was introduced in 2017 [29]. Different from the traditional laser cladding technology, HSLC directly acts the laser energy on the powder through the adjustment of powder focus and laser focus, so that most of the energy are absorbed by the powder above the substrate, which improves the cladding efficiency and reduces the dilution effect. Therefore, the thermal deformation of the substrate is slight, and the residual stress inside the coating is small and is less prone to cracking. The advantages of HSLC technology are re­ flected in the following aspects: (1) The cladding rate is 100–250 times higher than that of traditional laser cladding technology; (2) The surface roughness is low, and finish machining can be carried out directly in the later stage; (3) It has little thermal effect on the substrate and small surface deformation of the substrate; (4) There is metallurgical bonding between substrate and coating. As a new surface coating technology, it retains the advantages and overcomes the shortcomings of the tradi­ tional cladding technology, therefore, it is of great significance to study the basic application of HSLC technology. Moreover, the decomposition of WC is related to the volume fraction of WC particles in the coating. Reasonable WC content can solve the problems of cracks and pores in the laser cladding process, and effectively improve the microstructure and properties. Therefore, the study of WC content has far-reaching significance. In this paper, the coating can be solidified in a very short time to ensure that WC particles stay in high temperature state for a short time according to the fast heating and fast cooling characteristics of HSLC technology, which can reduce the degree of melting and decomposition of WC particles, so the high hardness and high wear resistance of WC are retained in the maximum extent. The stress concentration can be decreased by reducing the decomposition degree of WC and evenly distributing in the coating, which can solve the problem of cracking of high content WC strengthened coating. In this paper, Ni/WC composite coatings were prepared on the surface of 304 steel by HSLC technology to investigate the effects of different contents of WC on the micro­ structure, hardness, tribology properties of the coating, and the mech­ anism of the effect are further to be discussed. powder (particle size 35–135 μm). Test methods In order to observe the microstructure of the coating, the samples were cut to 10 mm × 10 mm × 10 mm by the wire electrical discharge machining (WEDM), which were corroded with aqua regia for 15–35S after grinding and polishing. The microstructure of different areas of the samples and the bonding between the coating and the substrate were observed by Axiovert 40MAT metallographic microscope and ZEISS Sigma300 scanning electron microscope. The phase composition of the coating was characterized by X ’Pert PRO X-ray diffractometer. The coating hardness was measured by Micromet-6030, Chain micro-Vickers hardness tester with a load of 500 g and a loading time of 10 s. Hardness tests were performed from the coating surface to the substrate, selecting five points horizontally in each area, and taking the average value of the five groups of data as the hardness value of the area. Remove a maximum value and a minimum value from the hardness value of the coating, and take the average value as the average hardness of the coating. The specific test method is shown in Fig. 2. The dry sliding friction and wear test were carried out at room temperature by friction and wear tester (BRUKER UMT-5). The grinding ball was made of Si3N4 with diameter of 6 mm, the 40 min test time, the 30 N load, meanwhile, the reciprocating friction length was 5 mm and the frequency was 2 Hz. Optical profiler (BRUKER Contour GT-K) and ZEISS Sigma300 scanning electron microscope were used to detect and analyze the wear trace morphology. Wear resistance Fig. 14 shows the variation of friction coefficient with time for the Ni/WC composite coatings. As shown in Fig. 14 (a), the friction curves of the seven component coatings all experiences running-in stage and the stable wear stage. In the running-in stage, the friction contact surface is relatively smooth. At this time, the contact mode of the grinding ma­ terial is point contact, and the contact stress is large, leading to the in­ crease of friction coefficient. Then it gradually enters the stable wear stage, and the contact mode changes from point contact to surface contact, as a result, the contact stress is gradually reduced, resulting in a stable friction coefficient. As shown in Fig. 14 (b), the friction co­ efficients decrease with the addition of WC, and the friction coefficients of B-E coating are kept at about 0.5 and its fluctuation is not obvious. The friction coefficients of F and G coating fluctuate around 0.4. Moreover, it is found that the friction coefficients of coating B-G increase at first and then decrease. The reasons for this phenomenon may be as follows: (1) It has been reported that the friction coefficient will increase when WC content increases from 15% to 35% [34], so it is consistent with the phenomenon that friction coefficients increase when WC con­ tent increases from 10% to 30% in this paper. The melting damage of some WC particles leads to the increase of friction coefficients; (2) Relevant studies confirm that the friction reduction effect of WC parti­ cles with small size is much higher than that of large size [35], and there are many small size WC particles in coatings with high WC content in this paper, so the friction coefficient decreases when WC content in­ creases from 30% to 60%.
