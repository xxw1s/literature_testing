The gradient coating with a thickness of about 420–490 mm contains two different layers, e.g. a bright layer with coarse equiaxed grain and a dark layer with ﬁne and columnar dendrites, and a transition layer with a thickness of about 10 mm exists between the substrate and the cladded coating. NiTi, NiTi2 and Ni3Ti intermetallic compounds are the main constructive phases of the laser cladded coating. The appearance of these phases enhances the microhardness, and the dense structure of the coating improves its oxidation resistance. Introduction Titanium alloy is one of the most important aerial materials for its high strength-to-weight ratio and excellent corrosion resistance, however, titanium is easily oxidized at high temperature for the high chemical active of titanium element. In order to improve the oxidation or wear behavior of pure titanium and its alloy at high temperature, many methods have been studied and the surface modiﬁcation treatment has been recognized as an effective method by more and more researchers. Until now, a lot of new surface modiﬁcation technologies have been used to the protection of pure titanium and titanium alloy, e.g. hot spraying [1], pack cementation [2–3], plasma surface alloying [4–5], PVD or CVD [6– 7], sol-gel method [8] and laser cladding [9], among which laser cladding is an advanced surface modiﬁcation treatment technology using high power laser to re-melt and obtain a functional coating with metallurgical bonding with the substrate. With its high efﬁciency, high ﬂexibility and high stability, laser cladding can be used widely in many ﬁelds. And for the metallurgical bonding of laser cladded coating and the substrate, their bonding strength is higher than that of the coatings fabricated by those methods, and better wear and corrosion resistance can be achieved. For the appearance of Cr, Fe, Si, Ti and other elements the cladded coating was reinforced by many strengthening mechanisms and the coatings perform a comprehensive property, such as higher microhardness and better resistance to sliding wear. Although the Ni-base coating was proved to be beneﬁcial to the improvement of the wear of the substrate, their large different of Ni and Ti in thermal physics make them be apt to separate. Meanwhile, for the appearance of Si and B elements with low melting point, the coatings are easy to overburning and are adverse to promote the oxidation resistance of higher temperature. To obtain the required multi-properties, coatings with single structure are unacceptable to some certain conditions, so gradient coatings with multi-layer structure have been designed and fabricated by many methods. Man et al. The coating was crack-free and exhibited obvious gradient variation in chemical composition and microstructure, which is good for the bonding of the coating and the substrate and expected to efﬁciently take the advantages of both the mechanical properties of titanium and the biological performances of HA ceramic. Above statements show that, to obtain an ideal protective coating with a higher bonding strength, laser cladding is avail and gradient coating is an ideal selection. In this paper, gradient coating was prepared by laser cladding on the surface of TA2 titanium alloy with different cladding parameters, and one processing condition which is the optimized processing parameters is used for the analysis of microstructure. The microstructure, phase constitution, solidiﬁcation procedure of the coating, the microhardness and oxidation morphology of the laser cladded coating are systemically studied. 2. Experiment The laser cladding of Ni based alloy coating on TA2 titanium alloy was carried out on a LRF-II model laser solid forming system, which consists of a PRC2000 continuous wave CO2 laser, a numerical control machine, a controlled atmosphere chamber and a powder feeding system with a coaxial nozzle. The TA2 titanium alloy plate with a size of 140 mm  50 mm  6 mm (length  width  thickness) was used as the substrate, and its chemical composition is presented in Table, 1. The TA2 titanium alloy substrate surface was polished with sand papers, and then was cleaned thoroughly with acetone before laser cladding processing. To obtain a nickel based coating, plasma rotation electrode processed (PREP) powders of GH4169 alloy with size of  150 mm and spherical shape were remelted and mixed with liquid titanium alloy in molten pool. The chemical composition of the GH4169 powder is listed in Table, 2. The parameters of laser cladding processing based on many optimized experiments were chosen as follows: laser power P¼1000, 1200 and 1400 W, scanning speed V ¼300, 400 and 500 mm/min, focal length L¼ 300 mm, spot diameter D ¼2.5 mm, width of single track d ¼1.6 mm, overlap η ¼ 50%, carrier gas speed of 30 L/min, powder feeding speed of 5 g/min and protective gases speed of 4 L/min (After one track was ﬁnished in longitudinal direction, the laser beam together with the powder feeding nozzle were moved a distance of 0.8 mm in transverse direction). Single trace of different laser cladding parameters is formed ﬁrstly to obtain a certain range of forming parameters, and then nickel based coatings with an area of 50 mm  30 mm were cladded with different laser cladding parameters varied in laser power and scanning speed. During the laser cladding process, the substrate was fastened onto a ﬁxture with bolts and pressed compacts to avoid the deformation of the substrate. After the laser cladding processing, the TA2 titanium alloy plate together with the holder was immediately put into a furnace with a presetting temperature of 500 °C and furnace cooling to room temperature. Metallographic specimen was prepared with standard metallographic practice in a cross-section vertical to the laser scanning direction. Microstructure of the coating The morphologies of single trace and microstructure characteristics of coatings prepared with different parameters are listed in Table 3. By comparison of single traces it shows the inﬂuence of laser cladding parameters on macro morphologies. As shown in Table 3, laser power and scanning speed have signiﬁcant effect on the formation of single trace and also form different microstructure characteristics of the coatings. Along with the increasing of laser power from 1000 W to 1400 W, the penetration of molten pool increased and the penetration ratio increased also. And they shows an opposite tendency when the scanning speed increased from 300 mm/min to 500 mm/min. To obtain a useful protective coating, some basic factors should be possessed by the coating, e.g. free of porosity and crack, perfect bonding with base metal and certain thickness which can give sufﬁcient protection. Seen from Table 3, when the laser power is low or the scanning speed is high, the thickness of the coating and the bonding to the base metal are good to be accepted, but a lot of incomplete fusion powders exist on its surface. When the laser power increased to 1400 W, a higher penetration ratio appears and more Ti element will go into the coating, which will increase the possibility of forming too much intermetallic compounds and cracks. After comparing the microstructure characteristics shown in Table 3, a medium level of laser cladding parameters has been selected as the optimized condition, and the corresponding coating was given future analyze to draw out its microstructure and oxidation resistance. The surface of the cladded coating is smooth and no cracks and pores are detected. Phase constitution of the coating To identify the phase constitution of the laser cladded coating, XRD analysis is conducted and the results are shown in Fig. 4. Intermetallic compounds such as NiTi, NiTi2 and Ni3Ti are the main constitutions of the outmost surface in the laser cladded coating. However, small amount of Ni is still detected in this area which means some GH4169 alloy powders are adhered on the surface of the coating during laser cladding. In a layer with a distance of 0.23 mm to the surface, similar phases are detected, however, the height of the diffraction peaks are much lower than that of the outmost surface, which means the lower quantities. More closer to the substrate, fewer intermetallic compounds are detected as seen by the diffraction peaks. In a layer with a distance of 0.4 mm to the surface, only diffraction peaks of NiTi2 can be detected, and this area is near to the substrate. It should be pointed out that the diffraction peaks of intermetallic compounds in the upper layers are much higher than that of the substrate. The reason is the directional orientation of grain and larger grain size, as shown in Figs.1 and 2. Inferred from the chemical composition analysis shown in Table 1, only NiTi2 phase, NiTi phase and β Ti phase are detected in the coarse equiaxed dendrite structure of the coating. However, seen from the XRD analysis results, Ni3Ti phase and small amount of Ni phase are presented. The Ni phase is coming from the GH4169 alloy powders adhered on the surface of the coating, and Ni3Ti phase is mainly presented in the upper regions of the coating where Ni element content is high. 3.3. Solidiﬁcation procedure of the coating There are two directions along with the heat dissipates away, one way is along the top-down direction with which the heat dissipated through the substrate, and another way is along the surface direction with which the heat dissipated through the already solidiﬁed regions and surface of the coating [3,9]. The solidiﬁcation patterns of the molten pool depend on their heat dissipation patterns. It can be detected that in this experiment the heat is mainly dissipated from the substrate. The solidiﬁcation occurred in the bottom of the molten pool is much rapid for the lower temperature of the substrate and the higher temperature gradient, and it is only presented in the dark layer within a thickness of about 20 mm as shown in Fig. 1d. And seen from Fig. 2b and c, the solidiﬁcation of liquid metal in this region is divided into two steps. In the ﬁrst step β Ti phase forms with a congruent reaction in form of cellular structure as shown in Fig. 2c, and in the second step (NiTi2 þ βTi) eutectic phases are formed with a eutectic reaction in form of ﬁner dendrite structure as shown in Fig. 2b. However, unlike the laser additive manufacture of metal materials where a typical coarse columnar grain exists for the epitaxial continuous growth of dendrites [10], a large amount of equiaxed dendrites exist in the laser cladding coating. The formation of the equiaxed dendrites region is due to the heat dissipation pattern and the chemical compositions of this area. Microhardness of the coating Fig. 6 shows the microhardness variations along a line from the surface of the coating to the inner part of the TA2 substrate. An average value of about 721HV0.2 in the coating and 158HV0.2 in the substrate are detected and the laser cladded coating has a much higher hardness compared with the substrate. Moreover, the distributions of hardness in both the laser cladded coating and the substrate are all uniform as shown in Fig. 5. The uniform distribution of microhardness in the laser cladded coating means the uniform microstructures correspondingly. The change of hardness across the transition layer occurs in a distance of about 100 mm. A value of 585 HV is detected in the dark layer as shown in Fig. 1d, and it means that the phase consisted in this layer has a lower hardness than the bright layers as shown in Fig. 1c, and it can serve as a buffer when the coating is bearing a load. Two possible factors bring the rising of the microhardness in the laser cladded coating. One is the formation of intermetallic compounds in the coating for the mixture of Ni, Ti and other elements. As shown in Fig. 4, intermetallic compounds such as NiTi, NiTi2 and Ni3Ti are detected in the laser caldded coating, and these precipitates have a higher hardness. The other is the rapid solidiﬁcation of molten pool can capture more alloying elements in the solid solution phase of Ti and bring a much more effective solution strengthening. Seen form Fig. 4, intermetallic compounds possess higher volume fraction, and so that the rising of microhardness in the laser caldded coating is much related to the formation of intermetallic compounds. 3.5. High temperature oxidation of the coating The oxidation surface morphologies of the laser cladded coating and the substrate samples after exposed at 800 °C for 100 h are shown in Fig. 7. Titanium alloys are easily oxidized for the formation of various types of TiO2 oxides and their low density. When exposed at 800 °C, TiO2 layer which is not dense enough quickly forms on the outmost surface and then decomposes on the interface near to the titanium substrate, so that oxygen atoms can easily approach into the metal grain lattice through this transit layer [17]. Along the elongation of dwell time, oxidation ﬁlm becomes thicker and the morphology of the oxides on the surface changes correspondingly. Fig. 7a shows a typical morphology of TiO2 grain of the TA2 substrate after oxidation in which the growth of the grain is sufﬁcient, and some gaps can be found through which the oxygen atoms can penetrate into the substrate metal and accelerate the oxidation of the substrate. However, the density of the oxidation surface of the laser cladded coating is much higher than the TA2 substrate, as shown in Fig. 6b, even if the same phase of rutile type TiO2 is found by XRD analysis in these F. Meanwhile, it has been reported that Al element can diffuse from the matrix to the surface in titanium and titanium-aluminum intermetallics [18], which resulted in the formation of dense Al2O3 layer which can effectively inhibit the further diffusion of oxygen atom and enhance its oxidation resistance to a certain extent. Meanwhile, Ni-Ti intermetallic compounds are found in the laser cladded coating by XRD analysis and microstructure observation. NiTi, NiTi2 and Ni3Ti phases all have better corrosion resistance compared with pure titanium. 4. Conclusions (1) A gradient coating is obtained by laser cladding on the surface of the TA2 substrate. The whole cladded layer can be divided into two different layers and the chemical compositions of these layers are different. (2) The cladded coating mainly consistes of NiTi, NiTi2 and Ni3Ti intermetallic compounds. The microhardness of the laser cladded coating decreases signiﬁcantly from coating 1 to coating 2, while it is much higher than the substrate for the formation of intermetallic compounds. (3) The cladded coating performs a better high temperature oxidation resistance compared to the TA2 substrate for the formation of intermetallics compounds.
